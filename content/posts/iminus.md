Long time no seen, it feels good to be back blogging!

I'm currently on summer vacation in the US, more specifically the westcoast of Florida. We've been driving all the way down from NYC, which took us about one week, mostly because of sightseeing and the fact that we took our time.

I've seen so much this past week, and thought it would be cool to atleast give whoever is reading this a glimse into how the vacation is going :-).

So, as I was saying, we started in NYC. We were there for a couple of days, saw some cool stuff viz. Ground Zero, Central Park, the Edge (which the picture above was shot!). After that we rented some cars, which we've been driving all the way down to where we are now. On the way here we got a little taste the of the Blueridge Mountains, which gave us quite the contrast from the urban settlements we visited, viz. DC, Savannah and Asheville.

When we finally came down were we're currently staying, we've mostly been spending our time close to the house we're renting. It's quite strange how American households have everything on one floor, but it's indeed practical to get around the house because of it.

I'm currently, as of writing this, in what looks like a twin bedroom, coupled with two single beds and a spinning fan that is triggering some seizures in me hehe (only joking of course.). The curtains in this room are conspicuous, in that they quite old (not like retro-old, more like 70s-80s old, yuck.), with their red stripes, that cut into each other. 

Other then that I finished my bachelors degree in philosophy! So relieved that it's finally over. Although I'll miss philosophy, there's a difference between working *as* philosopher, and being *interested* in philosophy, and it has become apperent to me that I rather prefer the latter than the former. I don't want my playful spirit to get sick of philosophy, therefore I'll do my upmost to not excaust myself, and rather have it as side hobby. 

However, I'm not going to root myself to this statement, mainly because I want to hold my options open for the future; who knows, maybe I want to continue doing philosophy in like 10-15 years? Time will show.

I'm still waiting on a response from *Samordna opptak* if got into Cogntive Science in Bergen. I'm a bit stressed about the whole thing, mostly because I've already rented a apartment down there, so if I don't get in I'll most likely have to pay for the first month or two (which will sting a bit lol.). I'm hoping for the best though, and fortunatly I have some options in Trondheim if, for a lack of better words, *shit hits the fan*.

I think this will have to do for now. I'll try to remember that I'll post if I got in or not. As I've written above I'll hopefully get in, but there are no garanties in life. I really want to skip town though, atleast for a while, to get some new perspectives and impulses from some places other than where I've been living all my life.
